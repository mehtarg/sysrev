Walter 1989

Brief name

Effects of iron supplementation on infant psychomotor development

Recipient

"12-month-old infants…followed-up longitudinally since birth"

Why

"It was shown in a recent review [1] that previous studies…have failed to provide conclusive solution to the following questions:

(1) the severity of iron deficit necessary to influence behavior,

(2) the effect of the duration of iron deficiency,

(3) the effect of a short-term iron trial (before correction of anemia),

(4) the reversibility of changes after a long-term trial (sufficient to reverse anemia),

(5) the specific areas of mental or motor processes most affected, and

(6) the possible association of developmental deficits with behavioral patterns. To answer these questions, we describe a double-blind, placebo-control study in a cohort of 12-month-old infants...followed-up longitudinally since birth....the short-term iron therapy study was meant to compare the infant with himself as a control, not to establish the validity of the BSID [Bayley Scales of Infant Development] for age."

What (materials)

"All groups were advised to add fruit juices at 2 months of age, fruits at 3 months of age, a meat-vegetable soup and cereals at 4 months of age, eggs and legumes at 6 months of age, and table foods at 9 months of age."

What (procedures)

"The infants were observed with monthly clinical and anthropometric evaluations by physicians from INTA at the Community Health Center and weekly at their homes by field nurses performing dietary and morbidity surveys. At 9 and 12 months of age, venipunctures were carried out for the measurements of hemoglobin, hematocrit, mean cell volume, serum iron, iron-binding capacity, free erythrocyte protoporphyrin, and serum ferritin...Samples were obtained by venipuncture...after a 6-hour overnight fast. Hemoglobin, mean cell volume, and hematocrit were performed in a Coulter model ZBI (Coulter Electronics, Hialeah, FL), free erythrocyte protoporphyrin, in a hematofluorimeter (Aviv Biomedical Inc, Lakewood, NJ) serum iron and iron-binding capacity by a colorimetric method [2], and serum ferritin by radioimmunoassay (Gammadab Division of Travenol, Cambridge, MA). After the venipuncture at 12 months of age, the infants were tentatively allocated to an iron status group by the hematologist...The venipuncture performed at 9 months of age...to evaluate the progression of iron status 3 months prior to the developmental tests.

Within a week after the 12-month visit and venipuncture, each infant was brought to INTA for psychologic testing with the Bayley Scales of Infant Development (BSID)...On day 11, a repeat BSID was performed...
BSID [3]...measure of each child's development...infant behavior at 1 year of age...three components, the Mental Scale, Motor Scale and the Infant Behavior record. The Mental Scale...to assess memory, learning, vocalization, and language communication...determines the basis for abstract thinking...associated with "intelligence." [sic] the Motor Scale provides a measure for gross and fine coordination. These scales yield scores...that are age corrected so that the norm is 100 and the standard deviation 16...Infant Behavior Record (IBR) consists of 30 items clinically rated by the tester and does not yield a score...The first BSID was administered 7 to 10 days after the 12-month venipuncture by the same psychologist (C.G.P.)...unaware of the infant's iron status or therapy group...raw scores obtained at the first and second BSID administrations were converted to indices based on the child's age at the first interview."

Who provided

"...monthly clinical and anthropometric evaluations by physicians from INTA at the Community Health Center and weekly at their homes by field nurses performing dietary and morbidity surveys…The infants were seen regularly by the same physician in the clinic and the same nurse at the weekly home visits...BSID...psychologist"

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

Where

"...clinical and anthropometric evaluations by physicians from INTA at the Community Health Center"

When and how much

"...randomly allocated to four groups at 3 months of age…exclusively breast-fed were given a heme-iron fortified cereal or the solid food recommended by the health clinic. Those exclusively breast-fed were given either a an iron-fortified formula or the routine nonfortified milk provided by the National Supplementary Food Program...ferrous sulfate suspension (Fer-In-Sol, Mead Johnson, Evansville, IN) or placebo at a dose of 0.6 mL (15 mg of Fe2+ was given three times daily 1 hour before meals for the following 10 days."

Trial author

Soewondo 1989

Brief name

The effects of iron supplementation on performance in discrimination and oddity learning tasks

Recipient

"preschool children…iron replete, iron-depleted, and ID anemic."

Why

"This study is a double-blind, clinical nutritional trial to assess the functional consequences of iron supplementation in adult women and preschool children...Discriminatory learning (DL) measures attentional acquisition and extinction…assumption is that this test should detect a specific attention effect of disregarding environmental cues that are important for problem solving. Oddity learning…places little demand on attentional processes and taps conceptual ability."

What (materials)

"Pictures: two colored pictures cut from children's books and pasted on black poster board...Color-form figures: two stimuli were colored, geometric figures...red and blue...X and 0...Oddity learning (OL). Three pictures, two of which were identical, were presented simultaneously on a 7 X 18 black poster board...Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test. Forms A and B from the 1965 version [reference] of the PPVT...followed the instructions of the original test manual."

What (procedures)

"assessment of body-iron status…three hematologic surveys. The first…3 mo prior to iron supplementation; the second immediately before it…last, after the completion of the intervention….determined hemoglobin (Hb), hematocrit, serum ferritin, transferrin saturation (TS), free erythrocyte protoporphyrin (FEP), and vitamin A...Blood samples were collected by venipuncture. A small fraction from each sample was used to determine Hb, hematocrit, and FEP. The blood remaining...was allowed to clot in clean tubes...separation...serum was frozen and stored at —20 °C...
Hb...cyanmethemoglobin procedure with a Compur M® 1000 miniphotometer. Hematocrit...microhematocrit method with a Compur M® 1100 minicentrifuge. Serum iron...method defined by the International Committee for the Standardization in Hematology [4]; transferrin...endpoint radialimmunodiffusion (M Partigen transferrin kit); serum ferritin...radioimmunoassay method (Gamma dab 125I ferritin radioimmunoassay kit, Clinical Assay, Cambride, MA); and FEP was measured on blood specimens dried on filter paper...method described by [5]...


Cognitive functions
The battery of tests included two DL [discrimination learning] tasks, three oddity learning tasks, and the Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test (PPVT).

Two-choice discrimination learning (DL)
1) Pictures: two colored pictures cut from children's books and pasted on black poster board were presented to the child being tested. A happy face had been placed on the underside of the poster board on which one of the pictures had been pasted. The task was to discover which picture hid the happy face underneath it. After each trial the left-right position of the pictures was rearranged out of the child's sight. The procedure continued until a learning criterion of seven correct in a row was met; at this point a reversal shift was introduced into the problem, so that the correct stimuli were those that were previously incorrect...
2) Color-form figures:...identical to that for the pictures, except that the two stimuli were colored, geometric figures. Two colors were used — red and blue — and two forms—X and 0...colors were randomly paired on consecutive trials with the happy face, one of the forms (X,0) was consistently associated with the happy face...seven consecutive correct responses defined the learning criterion; at that point the reversal shift was introduced.

Oddity learning (OL). Three pictures, two of which were identical, were presented simultaneously on a 7 X 18 black poster board. The only instructions given to the child were to find the winner. The correct response was to point to the picture that was different from the other two. Feedback was given about response correctness...first series...new stimuli on every trial...remaining series...same stimuli were repeated twice or thrice. In each case the nature of the stimulus did not determine its correctness, but rather its relationship to other stimuli in the array.
Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test. Forms A and B from the 1965 version [6] of the PPVT were used...followed the instructions of the original test manual."

Trial author

Soemantri 1989

Brief name

The effects of oral iron supplementation on blood iron levels and learning achievement in rural Indonesian schoolchildren

Recipient

"...children from two primary schools in district Gunungpati"

Why

"...conclusion that iron supplementation on IDA children during a treatment period of 3 mo was able to improve hematological status, school achievement, growth velocity and morbidity. The objective of this study was to investigate the effect of 3-mo iron supplementation on the learning achievement of IDA children."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"A 12-mL venous blood sample was taken from each child by a physician and kept in a freezer kit. the samples were sent to the hematology laboratory in Semarang and analyzed on the same day they were collected. The analysis included determination of hemoglobin concentration estimated as cyanmethemoglobin; serum iron (SI), tested with a method modified by Bothwell and Mallet; total iron binding capacity tested by a method differing slightly from the technique originally described by Ramsay, that is, using sulfonated sathophenanthroline for colorimetric determination; and total protein albumin and globulin and calculated by dividing SI by TIBC and expressed as a percentage...The color progressive matrices [7] were employed to test general intelligence of the children in the study...clearly understood by the children...conducted before iron supplementation (T1), after the iron-supplementation period of 3 mo T2), and after an additional 3-mo period post supplementation (T3). An educational achievement test prepared by officials of the Department of Education and Culture at the local level was administered to all children. The multiple-choice test was distributed confidentially to the school teachers in the area...four main areas: mathematics, geology, social science, and language.The test was constructed by the teachers and a psychologist."

Who provided

"Both iron and placebo tablets were given by school teachers on school days and the distribution was supervised by paramedical personnel. Whenever necessary, social workers made home visits to deliver the tablets...An educational achievement test prepared by officials of the Department of Education and Culture...was constructed by the teachers and a psychologist."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"...tablets were given by school teachers on school days and the distribution was supervised by paramedical personnel. Whenever necessary, social workers made home visits to deliver the tablets...An educational achievement test prepared by officials of the Department of Education and Culture...was constructed by the teachers and a psychologist."

Where

"two primary schools in district Gunungpati"

When and how much

"10 mg ferrous sulfate per kilogram body weight per day for 3 mo…equals 2 mg elemental iron.

The placebo contained saccharin and tapioca and had the same size and color as the iron tablets…postintervention evaluations were conducted after an additional 3-mo period for all subgroups."

Tailoring

"All parasite-positive children were treated with pyrantel pemoate (combination) at a dosage of 10 mg/kg body wt for 2 d..stool examination...after 2 wk...repeated...Once the children were considered to be free of parasites they were randomly assigned"

Brief name

Effects of iron and folic acid supplementation on behavioural responses of young anaemic Indian boys

Recipient

"boys, all from a low socio-economic group and in the age-range 5-6 years"

Why

"...reported studies on anaemia and educational achievement have come primarily from the developed countries, with no information available on children in developing countries. Hence, we have assessed the cognitive behaviour of young children in two separate studies, before and after oral Fe-folic acid supplements"

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Initial values for height, weight, Hb and scores on psychological test were collected for all children in both the experiments. At the end of the period of supplementation values were obtained for Hb and psychological test performance...erythrocyte morphology was assessed initially...
Heights and weights were recorded using standard procedures [8]. Hb estimations were carried out...by the cyanmethaemoglobin method in Expt 2, in both instances using a photoelectric colorimeter [9]. Erythrocyte morphology was assessed from a thin blood smear stained with Leishman’s stain [9]...‘Draw-a-man’ [10] test adapted to Indian children by [11] was used to screen children for their initial IQ. Cognitive behaviour before and after supplementation period was assessed using the Indian adaptation of Wechsler’s (1 967) intelligence scale for children (WISC): this battery of tests consisting of six verbal and six performance tests was adapted for Gujarati children by [12]. The procedure was standardized with Gujarati children, primarily of the middle- and low-income groups. In the present study the tests were administered in Gujarati, the native language of the children."

Who provided

Where

When and how much

"....two tablets of Fe-folk acid daily for a period of 60 d. Each tablet consisted of 20 mg Fe and 0.1 mg folic acid.

The controls received sugar placebos…two divided doses…identical-looking sugar placebos"

Trial author

Pollitt 1989

Brief name

Effects of iron supplementation on IQ and educational attainment in children 11 years of age

Recipient

children aged 108 to 144 months without AE Bart or Hb H disease and without abnormal Hb E

Why

"...solidify the interferences made from the infancy data that ID. Has a deleterious developmental effect. An additional justification for the use of school achievement measures was the need to assess whether ID does indeed affect the process of learning in formal schooling...clinical trial...to assess the impact of iron treatment on the intelligence quotient and educational attainment of 9-11-y-old children."

What (materials)

"...children were informed of the nature and objectives of the study"

What (procedures)

"Skin infections, upper respiratory infections and gingivitis due to dental caries were treated and recorded but these conditions were not criteria for exclusion..Two 200-mg tablets of albendazole...anthelminthic drug...given to every student enrolled in the study on the day of the blood test at T1 and 3 mo after the day the intervention started...

Hematology.
15 milliliters of venous blood from the arm was drawn from each child by disposable plastic needles and syringes and placed into the EDTA tube for blood cell count and determination of thalassemia and/or hemoglobinopathies. A drop of blood was placed in sealed with vaseline in a colored slide stained with 1% Brilliant Cresyl Blue to demonstrate inclusion bodies for -thalassemia determination. Abnormal hemoglobin and thalassemia were determined...by hemoglobin electrophoresis by use of a cellulose acetate strip [13]. Diagnosis of -thalassemia was confirmed by micro column chromatography with DEAE-Sephadex a-50 (Pharmacia Fine Chemicals AB, Uppsala, Sweden) [14]. Any other hemoglobin abnormalities were confirmed with starch gel electrophoresis [15]. -thalassemia trait was determined by the positive inclusion bodies and percent of the Hb Barts which was calculated by solid phase two-site immuno radiometric assay...
Hb, hematocrit red-blood-cell and white-blood-cell counts and mean cell volume were done by blood cell counter (Cell-Dyn 400® hematology analyzers, Sequoia-Turner Co, Mountain View, CA). A pediatric hematologist (PH) examined blood cell morphology. Serum iron and total iron binding capacity were measured according to the specific recommendations from the International Committee for Standardization in Hematology [4]. Transferrin saturation was calculated by the ratio of serum iron to total iron binding capacity , x 100. Serum ferritin was determined by double-antibody radioimmuno assay with a kit [16]. Free erythrocyte protoporphyrin was determined by the micro method [17].

Intellectual function and educational achievement. IQ, Thai language and math tests were administered in group form in the classrooms after the children were informed of the nature and objectives of the study. Two field researchers and a school teacher checked the IDs, distributed the test booklets and answer sheets, supervised the subjects throughout the test, and collected the completed forms.
The Raven Colored Progressive Matrices (RCPM) [reference], forms A, Ab, and B, were used to measure general intellectual function (IQ). The educational achievement test for Thai language and mathematics (math) were modified from the Thai government's Ministry of Education instruments used in the public school system. Each test was divided into two parallel forms based on the odd (O) and even (E) items of the original test forms. Half of the sample took the O form before the treatment (T1) and the F form after treatment (T2) while the remaining half took the tests in the converse sequence (E,O). Children taking the same forms were not placed next to each other...


Socioeconomic status. The parents responded to a questionnaire on graduated indices of social structure such as parental education, family income and size, land owned, quality of housing, and availability of electrical appliances. School teachers reviewed the questionnaires for completeness and helped illiterate parents to complete the questionnaires."

Who provided

"A pediatric hematologist (PH) examined blood-cell morphology...IQ, Thai language and math tests ...Two field researchers and a school teacher checked the IDs, distributed the test booklets and answer sheets, supervised the subjects throughout the test, and collected the completed forms...Socioeconomic status...School teachers reviewed the questionnaires for completeness and helped illiterate parents to complete the questionnaires"

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"IQ, Thai language and math tests were administered in group form in the classrooms"

Where

"16 elementary schools in Chon Buri, Thailand…IQ, Thai language and math tests were administered in group form in the classrooms"

When and how much

"The iron treatment consisted of a 50mg/d tablet of ferrous sulphate during the first 2 wk (2 mg elemental Fe*kg-1*d-1) and a 100-mg /d tablet (mg*kg-1*d-1) during the remaining 14 wk. The placebo was a tablet with sweet cassava powder and had a color and appearance similar to those of the iron tablet."
