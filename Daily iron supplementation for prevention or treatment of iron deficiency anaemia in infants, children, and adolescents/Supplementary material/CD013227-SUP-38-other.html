Trial author

Pereira 1978b

Brief name

Effect of daily iron supplements on the growth, activity and haematological status of preschool children

Recipient

"...apparently-normal children, 2-5 years of age, resident in an orphanage...Children with tuberculosis and other chronic diseases were not admitted. None of the children had hookworm infestation."

Why

"The present studies were undertaken as a preliminary trial to assess the effects of fairly small Fe supplements on the growth, activity and haematological status of preschool children."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"...each child was medically examined and a skiagram of the chest was obtained… Blood samples were taken without venous occlusion at the start of the study for estimation of haemoglobin, packed cell volume, serum Fe, unsaturated Fe-binding capacity, serum vitamin B12 and serum folic acid. Haemoglobin was estimated by the cyanmethaemoglobin method, using a photoelectric colorimeter checked periodically against an international reference standard (International Committee for Standardisation in Haematology). Values for packed cell volume were determined using a standardized microhaematocrit centrifuge. Serum Fe was estimated by the method recommended by the International Committee for Standardisation in Haematology [1]. The unsaturated Fe-binding capacity was determined by the method of Herbert, Gottlieb, Lau, Fisher, Gevirtz & Wasserman [2] and the serum vitamin B, assayed using Euglena gracilis Z strain [3]. Serum folic acid was determined by the technique of Waters & Mollin [4] using Lactobacillus casei as the test organism.

The heights of the children were measured at monthly intervals and were the average of three readings on each occasion. Weights were taken every 2 weeks on three consecutive days and the mean value recorded. The illnesses that occurred among the children were recorded every day by trained resident staff."

Who provided

"The illnesses that occurred...were recorded every day by trained resident staff. The activity of each pair of children was assessed at the beginning, at 3.5 months and at 7 months, during the morning and afternoon free-play sessions by the same two observers recording their activity (lying, sitting, standing, walking and running) at 5 min intervals throughout the total play period of 7 h."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"...trained resident staff"

Where

"orphanage"

When and how much

"...children in the experimental group were given daily supplements of 30 mg inorganic Fe [iron] as ferrous sulphate with 5 pg cyanocobalamin and 200 pg folic acid, while the children in the control group were given only the cyanocobalamin and folic acid supplements...for...12 months"

Trial author

Migasena 1972

Brief name

Effects of iron supplementation on haemoglobin and growth in Thai children 6 months to 6 years of age

Recipient

"Children with haemoglobin values below 10 gm per 100 ml blood and between the ages of 6 months and 6 years…from the resettlement village"

Why

"...dietary iron may either be poorly absorbed or unavailable in the food of the children or…other factors, eg., parasites were causing the anaemia.The effect of iron supplementation was studied therefore in some of these children to determine firstly whether the anemia was due to a dietary iron deficiency and secondly to determine whether the growth rate was influenced by the iron supplement."

What (materials)

"Standing height and weight (beam balance)"

What (procedures)

"ferrous sulphate syrup was prepared...identical control but containing no iron was also prepared...

Whole blood haemoglobin…determined either by the method of Sundharagiati and Harinasuta [5] or by the cyanmethaemoglobin method (Aculute reagent, Ortho Diagnostics Corp., U.S.A.). When using the latter, the absorbance of the samples was measured not more than 6 hours after dilution in Aculute. Haematocrit (PCV) values were determined by a micro-capillary method and mean corpusculuar haemoglobin concentrations (MCHC) were calculated from the results...Standing height and weight (beam balance) were recorded monthly."

Trial author

Fuerth 1972

Brief name

Effects of iron supplementation in full-term infants in their first year of life

Recipient

"...healthy, full-term infants…members of the Kaiser Health Plan"

Why

"...experience in private practice does not seem to bear this out. In a recent study by the author, the incidence of anemia in well babies was less than 5 per cent.[6]"

What (materials)

"The mother was then given just enough of the coded supplement to last her until the next visit...No special feeding advice was given to the study children, but most pediatricians in our group advised that ce- real be added to the daily diet between two to three months of age, meat between three and four months, and egg yolk by six months."

What (procedures)

"Hemoglobin was measured spectrophotometrically as cyanmethemoglobin. Blood for hematocrit determination was collected in heparinized capillary tubes, spun on the Drummond microhematocrit centrifuge, and calibrated on the Drummond microhematocrit reader. Serum iron and serum iron binding capacity were determined by a modification of the Ramsey method adapted to micro- technique [7].
At the first project visit a family history was obtained. At each visit thereafter the mother was asked to fill out a questionnaire concerning the baby's well being. Questions were asked about the child's daytime irritability, sleep pattern, feeding habits, and whether the child had been ill. She was also asked about any adverse side effects the child might have experienced from the medication."

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"At each of the project visits, made at 1, 3, 6, 9, 12, and 18 months of age, blood samples were collected by deep heel prick for hemoglobin, hematocrit, serum iron, and serum iron binding capacity determinations."

Where

—

When and how much

"Infants assigned to the iron supplement were given 0.6 c.c. of ferrous sulfate (Fer-in-sol, supplied by Mead Johnson Laboratories) twice daily (30 mg, of elemental iron per day). The control group received a placebo containing bismuth to color the stools."

Elwood 1970

Brief name

Evaluation of Iron Supplements in Prevention of Iron-Deficiency Anæmia

Recipient

"A 1-in-3 random sample of all the 14-year-old girls attending schools in the City of Cardiff"

Why

"The amount by which iron intake should be increased is unknown, and decisions seem to be based on very inadequate evidence."; "We describe here two experimental models for the evaluation of iron supplements"

What (materials)

"Packets of tablets"

What (procedures)

"Packets of tablets were sent by post every month and these contained either 0, 10, or 30 mg. iron, allocation being random. The tablets also contained a trace of riboflavine"

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

tablets; individual

Where

"schools in the City of Cardiff"

When and how much

daily; "0, 10, or 30 mg. iron"

Tailoring

—

Modification of intervention throughout the trial

—

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"The tablets also contained a trace of riboflavine, and this enabled spot tests to be made in which a sample of urine was checked for fluorescence under an ultraviolet lamp (Woods light)."

Extent of intervention fidelity

"The spot urine tests indicated that in the trial in adolescent girls, about 70% took a tablet each day during the first year. Unfortunately, we could not continue these tests during the rest of the trial but, from close questioning of the girls and some of their parents, we estimated that the proportion who took the tablets regularly subsequently fell to around 40%."
