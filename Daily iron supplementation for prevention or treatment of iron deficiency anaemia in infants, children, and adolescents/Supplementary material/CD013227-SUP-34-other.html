Thibault 1993

Brief name

"The immune response in iron-deficient young children: effect of iron supplementation on cell-mediated immunity"

Recipient

"...children aged 6 months-3 years undergoing a free systematic examination in four children's health centres from December 1989 to April 1990, in an area of Paris known for its low socio-economic level."

Why

"The effects of iron deficiency on immunity remain controversial. This study was designed to assess the impact of iron supplementation on the immune status, in 81 children aged 6 months-3 years, at high risk for iron deficiency, using a longitudinal double blind randomised and placebo-controlled study."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Anthropometric and prior vaccination data were obtained from the files of the health centres. Body composition was evaluated by the body mass index (weight/height 2) expressed as Z score for age [1]. Seven millilitres of whole blood were withdrawn by venipuncture at the start of the study and after 2 months. Haemoglobin, haematocrit, red blood cell count, mean corpuscular volume and white cell count were measured on a Coulter Counter S 560 (Coultronics, France). The white cell differential was assessed by microscopic examination of blood smears after May-Grünwald-Giemsa staining. Erythrocyte protoporphyrin measurement was performed by haematofluorometry. Serum iron was assayed colorimetrically and serum ferritin by an enzyme-linked immuno absorbent assay. Serum transferrin, C-reactive protein (CRP), orosomucoid, IgG, IgA and IgM were measured by nephelometry. Detailed procedures and percentage transferrin saturation calculation are described elsewhere [2]."

Trial author

Idjradinata 1993

Brief name

"...effects of dietary iron supplementation on performance in the Bayley scales of mental and motor development of IDA infants."

Recipient

"12-18-month-old infants in Indonesia"

Why

"Iron-deficient anaemic infants perform worse in tests of mental and motor development than do iron-sufficient infants of a comparable age. A randomised, double-blind trial was done to monitor the effects of iron supplementation on performance in the Bayley scales of mental and motor development among 12-18-month-old infants in Indonesia."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Infants were tested with the Bayley scales of mental and motor development 1 day before and immediately after the end of the experimental intervention...The infant behaviour record, which is also part of the Bayley scales, was completed for each infant by the tester at the end of testing. The infant behaviour record assesses, among other things, aspects of the infant’s social and emotional behaviour, attention, and persistence during testing.We explored the possibility that the low scores of IDA [iron deficiency anaemia] infants were mediated by motivational or state factors rather than by a lack of developmental competence."

Who provided

"Parents gave the syrups to infants at home. Compliance was checked weekly by a nurse...The Bayley scales were administered by three developmental psychologists from the Faculty of Psychology, Padjadjaran University. The psychologists were experienced with the administration of developmental scales and well informed on the theoretical basis of psychological testing."

Trial author

Boivin 1993

Brief name

Iron supplementation and cognitive performance in schoolchildren in Zaire

Recipient

"...first-year primary children…none of these children were HIV positive" boys and girls

Why

"...evaluate the effects of both deworming and iron supplements separately and in combination against the backdrop of the health, socioeconomic, and nutritional characteristics noted as being significantly related to physical development and nutritional status...to gain a preliminary estimate of the relative contribution of some of the above variables to any overall cognitive performance improvements in response to intervention."

What (materials)

"...the parents of the children were informed about what the students would undergo for both the medical and cognitive evaluations, and asked if they would be willing to have the children participate."

What (procedures

"The Mental Processing portions of the Kaufman Assessment Battery for Children (K-ABC; [3, 4]) was used...The format, administration, and scoring of the test was designed so that it would be readily adaptable to the diverse needs of preschoolers, minority groups, and exceptional children....Instructions and test items were administered in Kituba, a Bantu trade language common throughout the Bandundu region...The Zairian children were tested individually in a small classroom setting by one of five different Zairian schoolteachers trained in the administration of the instrument and enlisted as members of the research team...Although the first two mental processing subtests in the battery, Magic Window and Face Recognition, are intended only for children from 2 years 6 months to 4 years 11 months, these were deemed useful measures for the age group in our sample of Zairian children during pilot testing and were included in the study. However,scaled scores were not obtained for these measures nor were they included in the computation of the global scale measures... During the week of the cognitive assessment, children in the study underwent a medical evaluation at the small medical center in Matele. The laboratory analysis portion consisted of a microscopic examination of a stool specimen for evidence of intestinal parasites, particularly hookworm (ancylostomiasis or ankylostome) and roundworm (ascariasis or ascaris)...

Blood hemoglobin level was estimated using the Tallquist method of staining and colorimetric evaluation. In addition, white blood cell (leukocyte) count with a blood Film with Leishman's stain technique was measured and used as an indicator of low-grade infection. A screening test for sickle-cell trait was also conducted during the follow-up medical evaluation, since the illness caused by this trait can be accompanied by severe anemia. The Emmel method was used in which a blood film was taken from a constricted finger so as to limit oxygen to the red blood cells. The slide was sealed with Vaseline and examined immediately (40 X microscopic examination) for sickling (elongated red blood cells) and after 24 hours. This test was used to rule out the presence of sickle-cell disease among less healthy children in our present sample.... The parents) of each child in the study were interviewed by the member of the assessment team who lived at Matele. Interview questions pertaining to the child's medical history were adapted from Wilson [5]...parents were questioned regarding family size, parental educational level and literacy, aspirations of parents personally and for child, size of family dwelling, number of rooms, quality of cooking facilities, drinking water, and waste disposal facilities in proximity to home ,economic resources and personal family possessions, salary and employment level for parents, nutritional resources (including productivity of family garden and fields), and allocation of finances to the purchase of meat or fish on a weekly basis. The interview lasted approximately 1.5 hours...The assistants completing these interviews and evaluations were not aware of the treatment status of the children.... Following medical evaluation, the children who were positive for intestinal parasites were randomly selected for either a curative dose of Decaris (Tetramisole or Levamisole) consisting of two 150-mg tablets administered 4 days apart or two aspirin (placebo) similar in texture and size administered in the same manner."

Who provided

"initial physical examination by an American nurse and two Zairian nursing assistants...Kaufman Assessment Battery for Children ....Instructions and test items were administered in Kituba, a Bantu trade language common throughout the Bandundu region, and the examiners were all native speakers of Kituba...the senior investigator (M.J.B.) worked with a Zairian native speaker of Kituba and with an American missionary schoolteacher who has taught in Kituba and the closely related dialect of Kikongo (Bas-Zaire region) for 35 years...tested...by one of five different Zairian schoolteachers trained in the administration of the instrument and enlisted as members of the research team...assistants completing these interviews"

"iron supplement (20 mg Fe) for 30 days …

placebo treatments were not used in the case of iron treatment. Seventeen children received both Decaris and iron, 7 received only iron, 8 received only Decaris, and 15 children did not receive either intervention."

Trial author

Bhatia 1993

Brief name

Growth performance in anemia and following iron supplementation

Recipient

"children 3-5 years of age"

Why

"study was undertaken (a) to determine the growth status of moderately anemic and non-anemic (normal) young children living under similar environmental conditions, and (b) to evaluate the growth status of anemic and normal children before and after supplementation with iron."

What (materials)

The weights...using a two pan balanced...Triceps skinfold (SFT)...using Harpenden calipers"

What (procedures)

"A simple interview schedule was used for obtaining basic sociocemographic data from parents including income, family size, mother's educational level and environmental conditions. Data on hemoglobin (Hb), height (Ht), Weight (Wt), midarm circumference (MAC), and triceps skinfold (SFT) were collected from all children at the commencement of the study...Data on dietary intake was obtained from a randomly selected subsample...

Hemoglobin (Hb) was estimated by the Cyanmethemoglobin method [6]. Height was recorded using a vertical height rod to the nearest 0.1cm. The weights were recorded using a two pan balanced...to the nearest 25g. Midarm circumference (MAC) was measured to the nearest 0.1 cm at the midpoint between acromion and elbow of the left arm. Triceps skinfold (SFT) was measured at the same site as MAC, to the nearest 0.1 mm, using Harpenden calipers that exerted a constant pressure of 10 g/mm2 [7]...weight for age and weight for height were calculated. Dietary intake of iron was obtained by analysis of duplicate portions of all foods and beverages consumed over a 24 h period [8]."

Trial author

Hathirat 1992

Brief name

"Effects of an iron supplementation trial on the Fe status of Thai schoolchildren"

Recipient

"9- to 11-year-old children…(a) absence of beta thalassaemia A E Bart's or Hb H disease, (b) absence of Hb E"

Why

"...to test the effects of an Fe supplement in the form of ferrous sulphate administered to 9- to 1 I-year-old children in sixteen schools in the Chon Buri province of Thailand...In addition to the issue of effectiveness of treatment, the study addressed the following specific questions relevant to public health and policy.

1 Is the risk of Fe deficiency associated with social and economic status?

2 Are there any side effects to an Fe supplementation intervention among schoolchildren with and without Fe deficiency?

3 Is the school an effective delivery system?

4 Is an anthelminthic intervention as effective as an anthelminthic plus Fe intervention over a 3-month period?"

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Every child in the sample received a physical examination before (TI) and after (T2) experimental intervention. Clinical abnormalities such as skin infections, upper respiratory infections, ear infections, or gingivitis due to dental caries were treated and recorded, but the children with such medical problems were included.

Haematology. Venous blood (15 ml) was drawn from the arm and placed into the EDTA tube for blood cell count and determination of thalassaemia-haemoglobinopathies. A drop of blood was placed and sealed with petroleum jelly on a coloured slide stained with Brilliant Cresyl Blue (10 g/l) to demonstrate inclusion bodies for alpha-thalassaemia determination. Abnormal Hb and thalassaemia were determined in every case by Hb electrophoresis using a cellulose acetate strip (Rigas 1956). If beta-thalassaemia was suspected the diagnosis was confirmed by microcolumn chromatography using DEAE- Sephadex a-50 (Toewsiri 1977). Any other Hb abnormalities were confirmed with starch gel electrophoresis (Gammack 1960). The alpha-thalassaemia trait was determined by the positive inclusion bodies and the percentage of Hb Bart’s which was calculated by solid- phase two site immunoradiometric assay (Sanguansermsri et al. 1987). The methods used for the determination of Hb, packed cell volume, erythrocyte and leucocyte counts, and mean cell volume as well as those for serum Fe, total Fe-binding capacity, transferrin saturation (TS), serum ferritin (SF) and free erythrocyte protoporphyrin (FEP) have been reported (Pollitt 1989)...Socio-economic variables. A questionnaire on graduated variables of social structure (e.g. parental occupation, education, income and wealth) was completed by the child with assistance from a parent or adult at the child’s residence. In instances where the parents were illiterate the questionnaires were completed with the teacher."

Who provided

"Socio-economic variables...completed by the child with assistance from a parent or adult...instances where the parents were illiterate the questionnaires were completed with the teacher...The tablets were distributed after lunch each day by the teacher...During the 2 week vacation each child was sent home with tablets and paper to record tablet intake."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"tablets were distributed by the teacher"

Where

"sixteen schools in the Chon Buri province of Thailand…in non-malarial areas…200 km from Bangkok...questionnaire on graduated variables of social structure...completed...at the child’s residence."

When and how much

"The Fe supplement consisted of one daily tablet of 50 mg FeSO4 (about 2 mg/kg body-weight) for the first 2 weeks, and a 100 mg tablet daily for the remaining 14 weeks. The placebo tablet contained powdered cassava and had a colour, size, and shape similar to the Fe tablet...No systematic provision of either tablet was planned for 1 d school holidays."

Tailoring

"The Fe supplement consisted of one daily tablet of 50 mg FeSO4 (about 2 mg/kg body-weight) for the first 2 weeks, and a 100 mg tablet daily for the remaining 14 weeks."

Trial author

Irigoyen 1991

Brief name

"Randomized, placebo-controlled trial of iron supplementation in infants with low hemoglobin levels fed iron-fortified formula"

Recipient

"...healthy, inner-city, minority, 6-month-old infants, fed iron-fortified formulas, with Hgb values ranging from 9 to 11.5 g/dL"

Why

"...it was of interest whether additional iron supplementation would enhance Hgb [haemoglobin] values…a determination whether iron supplementation beyond that provided by the diet actually improves the hematologic outcome in low-income infants with low-normal and low Hgb values is relevant to clinical and public health considerations. Our study addresses this question."

What (materials)

"Parents were instructed to give one dropper three times a day, before meals, and to continue feeding their infants iron-fortified formula throughout the study."

What (procedures)

"We scheduled three visits during the following 6 months. The mean age of the infants at entry into the trial was 6.3, SD 0.7 months…Parents were interviewed at each visit regarding socioeconomic, health, and dietary characteristics…Blood samples were collected by venipuncture, following standard practice at the hospital, in an estimated 5% of cases, samples were capillary...Hemoglobin concentration and red blood cell indices were measured in Coulter counter; EP was determined by chemical extraction [9] and FERR by radioimmunoassay (Ramco kit)."

Where

"...faculty practice at a teaching hospital in New York City...Blood samples were collected…at the hospital…scheduled three visits during the following 6 months…Parents were instructed to give one dropper three times a day"

When and how much

"...supplemental iron at 0, 3, and 6 mg/kg per day for 3 months"

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"An attempt to have mothers return the medication bottles resulted in too few to analyze. Compliance was measured by parental report at each follow-up visit; mothers were asked when they administered the medicine, how much they gave, how many bottles were left, and the amount left in each bottle."

Extent of intervention fidelity

No significant differences were found in compliance…among the three groups…Serial reports of compliance were consistent over time, with the exception of two mothers who reported more medication left at the last visit than that reported at a previous visit. Using parental report of how much medication was left in the bottle, 61% reported administering all; 35%, half or more; 4%, less than half; and 2% none."
