Reeves 1985

Brief name

Side effects from oral ferrous sulphate supplementation in infants one year of age

Recipient

"Healthy-appearing 1-year-old infants of military personnel seen in our well baby clinic between December 1981 and October 1983, were evaluated…Infants 11 to 14 months of age without anemia, history of premature birth, or chronic illness were eligible to enter the randomized study"

Why

"Because adverse side effects represent a major potential limitation to wider use of therapeutic trials, we studied the relative frequency of reported side effects among healthy infants being tested for possible iron deficiency with therapeutic trials of iron."

What (materials)

"Parents were advised to pay particular attention to possible side effects of treatment and to call the laboratory technologist about these even if they decided to discontinue participation in the study."

What (procedures)

"infants were tested for possible iron deficiency with venous blood measurements of hemoglobin, hematocrit, mean corpuscular volume, erythrocyte protoporphyrin, serum ferritin, serum. Iron, and iron binding capacity using standard laboratory techniques.[1]"

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

—

Where

"...well baby clinic"

When and how much

"...ferrous sulfate…drops, 1.2 mL orally every day (about 3 mg of iron per kilogram of body weight per day, 30 minutes before breakfast on an empty stomach) or equal volumes of placebo. Placebo contained water, sucrose, and sorbitol with trace amounts of alcohol, flavoring, preservatives, and dyes...also present in the FeSO4 drops in similar proportions...3 months of treatment"

Tailoring

—

Modification of intervention throughout the trial

—

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"Compliance was estimated by noting the volume of unused medication at follow-up."

Extent of intervention fidelity

"Approximately one third of each treatment group failed to complete the study. Most did not finish because they moved and were lost to follow-up or because they changed their minds about having further venipuncture blood tests."

Trial author

Gopaldas 1985

Brief name

The effect of prophylactic haematinic supplementation on selected tests of cognitive function in school age children

Recipient

"From a total of 210 subjects (8-15 years) participating in a larger study, a careful match in triplets was made of age, Hb [haemoglobin] levels, and baseline scores in the cognitive function tests."

Why

To test the effect of prophylactic haematinic supplementation on selected tests of cognitive function in school age children

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Hb was estimated by the cyanmethemoglobin method [2] at baseline and…approximately four months later, towards the end of school term…Cognitive function test…Visual Recall, Digit Span, Mazes and Clerical Task…(a) Visual Recall. Ten items were placed on a board and covered. The subjects were permitted to see and memorize the items for exactly one minute, timed with a stop watch. The items were covered again and the subject was asked to recall the items. This test measured memorzing capcacity...(b) Digit Span. Two sets of numbers were...read out to the subject slowly and clearly by the investigator. The subject was then required to recall the numbers in both the forward and backward sequences. This test measured attention, short term auditory memory, and auditory sequencing [3]. (c) Mazes...taken from the Gujarati adaptation of the Weschler's Intelligence Scale for Children [3]...tested visual motor coordination and visual perception [3]. (d) Clerical Task. Each subject was required to encircle all the mantras in a standard script within 5 minutes. This tested discrimination and perception...All tests were pre-tested and standardized among similar types of children...administration of the 4 tests took about 25-30 minutes per subject."

Who provided

"...field investigators"

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"4 tests…were carried out in privacy with one subject at a time"

Where

"...free school under the Baroda Municipal Corporation"

When and how much

"Brown sugar tablet (placebo);

a Yellow tablet of ferrous sulphate (30 mg Fe);

and an Orange tablet (40 mg Fe)…

Distribution was done from the commencement of the school term at the level of one tablet/subject/day for 60 days at a stretch"

Modification of intervention throughout the trial

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"It was ensured that full dose was received by all subjects."

Trial author

Linpisarn 1984b

Brief name

Effect of iron supplementation on plasma ferritin in school age children

Recipient

"...subjects were drawn from three rural villages in Northern Thailand, namely Pha Bath Hau Tom…Doi-tao village…Mae-rim village"; "school children (aged 7—12 years)"

Why

"To date there have been no extensive surveys in Thai populations of iron deficiency assessed by ferritin concentration. The objectives of this study were, therefore, to use serum ferritin measurements to determine the prevalence of iron deficiency in a Thai population and to investigate the effect of iron supplementation in this population."

What (materials)

"The purpose of the study was fully explained to all participants so that their consent could be obtained."

What (procedures)

"Blood samples were taken by finger-prick and collected into heparinised capillary tubes. The samples were screened for malaria and thalassemia HbE type…and positive subjects were excluded from the study…
type of supplementation provided: placebo (control group), iron supplementation...iron tablets were in a form of ferrous sulphate...

Blood haemoglobin and haematocrit were measured on the same day. Specimens for plasma ferritin measurement were taken from the haematocrit tube and stored in a polypropylene tube at —20° until analysed...Plasma ferritin concentrations were determined by the sandwich /ELISA [enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay] technique using microtitre plates as described by [4]. The microtitre plates were read using a Titertek Multiskan spectrophotometer (Flow Laboratories Ltd, Irvine, Ayrshire, UK). Haemoglobin was determined photometrically as cyanmethaemoglobin [5, 6] Haematocrits were spun in an International micro-haematocrit centrifuge at a speed of 11 500 rpm for 5 minutes."

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"Not all members of a village received supplements at the same time."

Where

"...first village is inhabited by a vegetarian hill-tribe…other two villages are inhabited by non-vegetarians."

When and how much

"Doses of…120 mg for school children…each supplementation period lasted 3 months"

Tailoring

"Doses of iron were 60 mg for pre-school children, 120 mg for school children, 120 mg and 240 mg for adult women."

Modification of intervention throughout the trial

—

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"under supervision"

Extent of intervention fidelity

—

Das 1984

Brief name

Evaluation of Frequency and Dose of Iron and Other Hemantinics - An Alternative Strategy for Anemia Prophylaxis in Rural Preschoolers

Recipient

"175 apparently healthy children...between 6 months and 6 years of age"

Why

"to evaluate if administration of weekly or biweekly iron doses could be used as an effective measure in anemia prophylaxis programme"

What (materials)

"vermifuge in form of Piperazine citrate (Antepar Syp., Burroughs Wellcome & Co. (India) Pvt. Ltd., Bombay). Metronidazole (Giardyl suspension, IPCA Laboratories Pvt. Ltd., Bombay)"; "Bephenium hydroxynaphthoate (Hoopar, Cadila Laboratories, Ahmedabad)"

What (procedures)

"Children in group II received vitamin A and D in form of Adexolin drops (Glaxo laboratories Ltd., Bombay) containing600 iu of vitamin A and 100 iu of vitamin D per drop. Group III children received vitamin B12 and folic acid in form of syp. Macrafolin (Glaxo Laboratories Ltd., Bombay) by means of special droppers delivering approximately 0.35 μg of vitamin B12 and 35 μg of folic acid per drop. Both these above groups received the treatment 5 days a week. The groups IV-IX received iron in the form of Neoferrum infants (Duphar-Interfran Ltd., Bombay) drops, each ml (20 drops) containing 100 mg of elemental iron. The placebo group 1 received flavoured sugar syrup."

Who provided

"trained Anganwadi workers under the supervision of the investigators"

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

drops; syrup; individual

Where

"9 Anganwadi areas of the ICDS block Reotipur, District Ghazipur (UP)"

When and how much

"Children in group II received vitamin A and D in form of Adexolin drops (Glaxo laboratories Ltd., Bombay) containing 600 iu of vitamin A and 100 iu of vitamin D per drop. Group III children received vitamin B12 and folic acid in form of syp. Macrafolin (Glaxo Laboratories Ltd., Bombay) by means of special droppers delivering approximately 0.35 μg of vitamin B12 and 35 μg of folic acid per drop. Both these above groups received the treatment 5 days a week. The groups IV-IX received iron in the form of Neoferrum infants (Duphar-Interfran Ltd., Bombay) drops, each ml (20 drops) containing 100 mg of elemental iron. The placebo group 1 received flavoured sugar syrup."; "The medicines were administered irrespective of the meal timings as has been done in some similar studies on pregnant women sponsored by the WHO(6)."

Tailoring

"The children with "severe anemia i.e., those with hemoglobin 6.0 g/dl or less were excluded from the study on ethical grounds and received active .treatment."; "The children's morbidity 'pattern was recorded every week. Those having minor illnesses like loose motions, upper respiratory tract infection were treated appropriately, while those with serious illnesseswere excluded from the study and were referred to the hospital for treatment."

Brief name

Side-effects of daily iron supplementation in children 1 to > 6 y of age

Recipient

children with moderate iron deficiency (haemoglobin 8.5 to 12 g/dL)

Why

the study aims to show the rate of side-effects of bivalent iron salts in children and whether there are differences in the tolerance between the iron salts compared to placebo

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

venous or capillary blood was taken; haemoglobin was measured photometrically, haematocrit via micro-centrifuge; serum iron via Mercko-Test; transferrin via radial immuno diffusion, serum ferritin via Ramco testkit

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

parents were asked to provide the supplements daily before meals

Where

home-based supplementation

When and how much

Group A: 160 mg iron fumarate = 50 mg iron, 100 g ascorbic acid
Group B: 177.5 mg iron sulphate = 50 mg iron, 100 g ascorbic acid
Group C: 145 mg iron succinate = 50 mg iron, 100 g ascorbic acid
Group D: placebo; 300 mg lactose

Children below 2 y took the supplements only in a crushed form or not at all
