Trial author

Mejía 1988

Brief name

Effects of iron and vitamin A supplementation on haematological indices of anaemic children

Recipient

"...anemic children, of both sexes, aged 1–8y…attending seven different day-care centers…and a children's nutritional recuperation center…belonged to urban or rural families of low socioeconomic status."

Why

"...suggests that vitamin A deficiency impairs the utilization of Fe and that improving vitamin A nutriture may benefit the hematological condition of populations. Considering that mild vitamin A deficiency is an endemic problem to our region, we felt it important to investigate the effect of supplementing anemic children with vitamin A, with or without additional Fe therapy, on their hematological condition and Fe nutritional status."

What (materials)

"Parents were immediately informed about the anemic condition of their children and were asked to authorize the children's participation in the study by signing a written consent form."

What (procedures)

"At the beginning of the supplementation trial…and at 1… and 2…mo of treatment, the children underwent clinical and anthropometric evaluations. The existing clinical characteristics and the weight and length, or height, of each child at the time of the examination were recorded in a standardized fashion on a special form...
a 5-mL venous blood sample was obtained from each child at t0 and at the end of the 2-mo supplementation (t2). The following blood analyses were performed: 1) hemoglobin, hematocrit, red cell count, hematological indices, and leucocyte count by standard methods using an automated cell counter (Royco-cell Crit®, model 920/A, HIAC/Royco Instument, Menlo Park, CA); 2) serum retinol by the ultraviolet inactivation method proposed by the International Vitamin A Consultative Group (IVACG) [1]; 3) serum Fe, total Fe binding capacity (TIBC), and percent transferrin saturation (% TS) by the Ramsay method [2]; 4) serum ferritin by an immunoenyzmetric assay with a commercial kit (Tandem-E Fer®, Hybritech Inc, San Diego, CA); and 5) erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) by the method of Wintrobe [3].
kept a record in each center of the morbidity of the children throughout the study period. The different illnesses were categorized as upper-respiratory infections, gastrointestinal infections, dermal infections, and others. This information was recorded in a special morbidity form each time there was a morbid event."

Who provided

"All three supplements were provided by the project and the supplementation was performed by the auxiliary nurses of each center with the supervision of the investigators. When possible the appropriate supplements were given to the parents on Friday afternoon for supplementing their children over the weekend."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"...supplements were provided…by the auxiliary nurse of each center with the supervision of the investigators."

Where

"...seven different day-care centers…and a children's nutritional recuperation center…Three of the centers…were located in Guatemala City. The others…were located in smaller cities throughout the country."

When and how muc

"...four different treatment groups to be supplemented orally for 2 mo with vitamin A (G-I), Fe (G-II), vitamin A plus Fe (G-III) or a placebo (G-IV)…

Group I. Supplemented with 10 000 IU (3.0 mg) of vitamin A/d in the form of a water-soluble preparation (Arovit®, Hoffman-La Roche, Basel, Switzerland). Each drop of the supplement contained 5000 IU (1.5 mg) of vitamin A...Therefore, two drops of the vitamin A preparation were used to ensure that the children received at least 5000 IU (1.5 mg). The drops were put directly into the mouth of the child immediately after the breakfast meal.
Group II. Supplemented with ferrous sulfate in the form of a syrup (Fer-In-Sol®, Mead Johnson, Evansville, IN) at a level of 3 mg of elemental Fe * kg body wt -1*d-1...the required volume ...was divided into two doses which were put directly into the mouth of the child...at midmorning and midafternoon.
Group III. Supplemented with both vitamin A and Fe as indicated for groups I and II.
Group IV...control group...placebo consisting of a solution that simulated the Fe preparation...composed of distilled water (800 mL), ethanol (200 mL), thymol blue (10 mL), and 0.5 mol sodium hydroxide/L (3—5 drops)...into the mouth...twice a day at midmorning and midafternoon."

Trial author

Chwang 1988

Brief name

Iron supplementation and physical growth of rural Indonesian children

Recipient

children 8.2 to 13.5 y of age with and without anaemia

Why

"...there is a scarcity of information on the effects of Fe supplementation on the physical growth of infants and children. This paper reports the effects of Fe supplementation on the physical growth of school-age children from a nutritionally at-risk rural population."

What (materials)

"...height...horizontal platform against a measuring tape"

What (procedures)

"...body weights were recorded to the nearest 0.05 kg"; height was measured with children standing "on a horizontal platform against a measuring tape…a reading was taken to the nearest 0.05 cm"; arm circumference was measured in subjects who "stood with their left am flexed 90°...midway between the acromion and the olecranon with the tape in contact with the skin. A reading was taken to the nearest 0.05 cm...
growth measures were transformed into sex- and age-specific Z scores...
Hb concentration was estimated as cyanmethemoglobin [4]. Serum iron...tested with a method modified by Bothwell and Mallet [5]. Total Fe-binding capacity...tested by the method differing slightly from... Ramsay [6]...using bathophenantroline for colorimetric determination." Transferrin saturation was computed by dividing serum iron by TIBC and expressed as percentage.

"Information regarding signs and symptoms of disease and illness were recorded daily, 2 wk before, and 2 wk after the child was referred to the pediatrician (AGS) who made the medical diagnosis as appropriate. Diseases were categorized as acute (dysentery, gastroenteritis, parotitis, and urinary tract infection) and chronic (rhinitis, tuberculosis, and tonsilopharyngitis). The severity of a disease was scored on a scale of 1—3...Children without symptoms were scored 0, children who were advised to refrain from attending school were scored 3. Disease duration was scored on a scale of 1—3, with 3 indicating that the disease lasted ≥ 3 days...The sum of values from severity and duration provided a total morbidity score...
the children’s susceptibility to infection was assessed by measuring the Fe-containing microbicidal enzyme myeloperoxidase (MPO) by the Kaplow method [7] with benzidine dihydrochloride as the staining indicator. MPO activity...was scored on a scale of 1-3, denoting the quantity of blue granules in every 50 granulocytes as large, few, and none, respectively."

Who provided

weight, height, and arm circumference were measured by two female physicians; "A 12-mL venous blood sample was drawn from each child by a physician."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"Fe and the placebo tablets were given by the school teachers on school days;"

Where

"...three villages in the subdistrict of Kalibawang in Central Java"

When and how much

"Fe or placebo treatment for 12 wk"

Tailoring

"...all parasite-positive children were treated with pyrantel pamoate...at the dosage of 10 mg .kg-1 . d-1 for 2 d."

Modification of intervention throughout the trial

—

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

"...tablets were given by the school teachers on school days; therefore, there was careful supervision of treatment compliance."

Trial author

Kashyap 1987

Brief name

Impact of iron supplementation on cognitive function in underprivileged girls

Recipient

"...subjects with recorded birth dates between 8-15 years, with family incomes not exceeding Rs. 500/= (US 42.00) per month and those whose parents gave informed consent for participation in the study...pairs of subjects were initially matched for age, Hb, individual and total CF [Cognitive Function] test scores."

Why

"There is clinical evidence to demonstrate that patients with iron deficiency have altered behavior, characterised by apathy, irritability, inability to concentrate and impaired visual motor coordination. Most of the studies conducted on behavioral changes associated with nutritional anemia have either been on animals, infants or preschoolers [8]. There is a great paucity of data on school children, especially girls...no study to our knowledge has been conducted on the changes in CF test scores on hematinic dosing in underprivileged school girls...onset of menarche increase the iron requirements in school girls to higher levels than those of boys of the same age group. Thus, to improve the hematological status of these future mothers, it is essential to meet their present iron requirements and build up adequate stores for the future needs...investigates the effect of a suitable prophylactic hematinic dose on underprivileged school girls...to study the impact of hematinic supplementation on (i) percent prevalence of anemia; (ii) Hb status; and (iii) other parameters of iron status, namely, serum iron (SI), total iron binding capacity (TIBC) and transferrin saturation (TS)."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"Hb data was obtained using the filter paper technique [9] of the cyanmethemoglobin method [10] at all four points of time.

Cognitive Function (CF) tests
A battery of four tests was selected, which included two tests from the Gujarati adaptation of Weschler's Intelligence Scale for Children (G-WISC)[11]...

Clerical Task (CT) test. Each subject was given a printed text in Gujarati and was instructed to encircle all 'matras' ( vowels 'a' and 'o' of Gujarati) in the entire text as quickly as possible over a 5 minutes period. The score was calculated as a ratio of the number of matras encircled to the total number of matras in the text. This was then multiplied by i0, which was the maximum obtainable score. The CT test was used to test attention, concentration and discrimination.
2. Visual Memory (VM) test. Ten items of everyday use were displayed to the subject for a period of one minute. The subject was required to recall as many items as possible in the next one minute, testing the ability to remember non-verbal (visual) material.
3. Digit Span (DS) test. Series of digits gradually increasing in length were presented to the subjects, who were to repeat the series in the correct order, vocally. Different series were presented for the forward and backward recall. The series for the forward recall started from 3 up to 9 digits and for backward recall 2 to 8 digits. The longest group of digits the subject recalled in correct order, determined the memory span for digits.
4. Mazes test. A series of line mazes in the increasing order of difficulty were presented to the subject who was to mark the way out within a stipulated time and within a set number of errors permitted [11]" for each Maze. A test intended to measure foresight planning capacity (perception) and visual motor coordination...The scoring for DS and Mazes tests were as per the WISC manual [11] but for simplicity of final scoring, a ratio of the score obtained, to the maximum score, was obtained. This was then multiplied by 0, which now became the maximum obtainable score (just the way percentage is calculated)."

Who provided

—

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"...data was collected every four months ie. baseline (0 month), at the initiation of the study; mid evaluation (4th month), at the end of the first school term; final evaluation (8th month), at the end of the second school term; and post final evaluation (12th month)...A battery of four tests was selected, which included two tests from the Gujarati adaptation of Weschler's [sic] Intelligence Scale for Children (G-WISC) [11]...These tests were carried out individually, in privacy to prevent any consultation between subjects and distraction to the study subjects."

Where

"Four schools, out of 45 primary schools catering exclusively to underprivileged school girls in Baroda city, Gujarat"

When and how much

"60 mg elemental iron (FeSO4) for 60 days at a stretch, twice in a school year...iron treated (60 mg elemental Fe/day)

or the placebo ( sugar tablets) group...However, chemical analysis of the Fe tablets by Wong's thiocyanate method [12] gave an analysis value of 62 mg elemental iron/tablet."

Trial author

Aukett 1986

Brief name

The effect of iron supplementation on weight gain and psychomotor development

Recipient

children with anaemia (Hb 8 to 11 g/dL) 17 to 19 mo of age

Why

"It is not clear, however, whether the iron deficiency causes the delay or merely the two are associated evidence of an underprivileged environment. Previous work on the use of iron supplements has yielded conflicting evidence…Also as they all used short-term treatment (seven to 15 days) the differences shown may have just related to short term behaviour changes."

What (materials)

"...children with anaemia then received either iron 24 mg (as ferrous sulphate) plus vitamin C 10 mg daily or vitamin C 10 mg alone daily."

What (procedures)

"Social, family, and medical history...was taken by the project nurses and interpreter as necessary and checked by one of us...using a standard form…

haemoglobin and red blood cell indices, haemoglobin electrophoresis, plasma iron concentration, which was measured colorimetrically using the Guanidine/Ferrozinemethod (JTBaker, Deventer, Holland) [13], transferrin, measured on a Baker Encore centrifugal analyser using anti-human transferrin (Hoecht UK Ltd, Hounslow, Middlesex)and calibrated with human transferrin (Boehringer Mannheim, West Germany), and ferritin estimation (Enzymeimmunoassay, Hybritch Europe SA, Liege, Belgium)...

Psychomotor development
The children were tested by one of us (AA or YP) with 24 items taken from the Denver developmental screening test [14] appropriate for the age group studied.

Who provided

"The normal clinic procedures…assessment of growth and development and physical examination…were carried out as usual by the community child health service staff. In addition, capillary blood for estimation of haemoglobin was taken by one of two project nurses...

Social, family, and medical history
This was taken by the project nurses and interpreter as necessary and checked by one of us...using a standard form...

Growth measurements were made by the project nurses"

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"One month's supply was given at the anaemia clinic. The project nurses visited every child at two weekly intervals to encourage compliance and to take a further supply of the supplement at the end of one month."

Where

"...catchment area of four child health clinics in central Birmingham"

When and how much

"...iron 24 mg (as ferrous sulphate) plus vitamin C 10 mg daily or vitamin C 10 mg alone daily…for two months"

Tailoring

"As far as we are aware the only variable in the way the children were handled was whether the supplement given contained iron or not, and this variation was double blind."

Trial author

Yip 1985

Brief name

Effect of iron supplementation on zinc status in healthy infants at one year of age

Recipient

"...healthy, term infants who were seen for their routine 1-yr health maintenance examination…All infants were dependents of Air Force personnel and had free access to medical care."

Why

"...findings suggest…iron supplementation might compromise the absorption of zinc. The purpose of this study was to determine whether administration of supplemental iron to healthy infants might result in zinc deficiency as manifested by a decrease in serum zinc."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"A 10 ml sample of venous blood (nonfasting) was drawn into trace element free tubes for laboratory studies which included Hgb, MCV [mean corpuscular volume], serum ferritin, erythrocyte protoporphyrin (EP), serum iron/iron binding capacity (Fe/TIBC), serum zinc, and serum copper...Three months later (±1 mo) at the 15 mo visit, laboratory studies on venous blood were repeated.
The methods were identical to those employed previously [15].
Hgb and red blood cell indices were measured on the Coulter Model S electronic counter (Coulter Electronics, Hialeah, FL) using the 4 -C tripak quality controls with each set of assays. Serum iron and iron-binding capacity were measured by AutoAnalyzer (Technicon Instrument Corp, Tarrytown, NY) using ferrozine (Sigma Chemical Corp, St. Louis, MO) as the color reagent. Serum ferritin was measured by a radioimmunoassay kit (Clinical Assays, Inc. Cambridge, MA) and protoporphyrin was determined fluorormetrically after extraction with ethyl acetate. Serum zinc and copper were measured by flame atomic absorption spectroscopy (Instrumentation Laboratories spectrophotometer model 551, Andover, Ma) [16]."

Who provided

"A form including information on dietary history was completed by the physician or nurse the time of the first visit between 11 and 13 mo of age…The parent was directed to administer the medication orally about 30 min before breakfast."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

"...parent was directed to administer the medication orally"

Where

"...a clinic that was specially set up for this study at the David Grant Medical Center, Travis Air Force Base, CA."

When and how much

"3 mo course of placebo or iron treatment (30 mg iron as ferrous sulfate given before a meal)."

Strategies to improve or maintain intervention fidelity

Trial author

Soemantri 1985

Brief name

Effect of iron supplementation on school performance among anaemic and non-anaemic children in an economically deprived rural area in Central Java, Indonesia

Recipient

"...children from...primary schools in Kalibwang…above 80th percentile of weight and height, and above 85th percentile of mid-arm circumference of Indonesian growth standards; negative parasite egg count by stool examination after treatment of ancylostomiasis; no evidence of hematological-related diseases..., other severe illness, nutrition deficiency, physical handicaps, and neurologcal abnormalities...; IQ above 75"

Why

"The present study investigates the effect of 3-mo iron supplementation on measures of school performance among iron-deficient anemic children in an economically deprived rural area in Central Java, Indonesia."

What (materials)

—

What (procedures)

"12 mL venous blood samples was taken form each child…sent to the hematology laboratory in Semarang and analyzed on the same day the blood sample was collected. The analyses included determinations of Hgb, Hct, serum iron, total iron binding capacity, total protein, albumin, and globulin. Serum ferritin data on a few number of children were also obtained...are not part of the present analysis.
Raven progressive matrices. Colored progressive matrices [17] were employed to test general intelligence of the children in the study...nonverbal IQ test...instructions were clearly understood by the children...The original Raven test was adapted for the rural population by the staff of the Psychometric Department, Factulty of Psychology of Grajah Maja University, Yogyakarta. This test was administered before the treatment.
Educational achievement test...included four main subject areas: mathematics, biology, social science, and language. The test, prepared by staff from the Secretary of Education in Semarang, was revised and short version of the standard achievement test used by the public school system. Selection of items for inclusion in the revised version was based on the prediction and discriminatory power of the test items. Accordingly, the construction of the test was biased towards the maximization of differences between high and low educational achievers...
Concentration. The Bourden Wisconsin test for concentration was employed both before and after the iron and placebo intervention."

Who provided

"...iron and placebo tablets were given by school teachers and supervised by paramedical personnel every morning during school days…Whenever necessary, social workers made home visits to deliver the tablets…Blood specimens were collected…by four study physicians and assisted by a laboratory technician."

How (mode of delivery; individual or group)

—

Where

"...subdistrict of Kalibawang...three schools because of logistic and budgetary reasons. These three schools had similar health programs, and the teachers were willing to cooperate."

When and how much

"...period of 3 mo on oral treatment of ferrous sulfate at a dose of 10 mg/kg/day, which is equal to 2 mg of elemental iron.

The remaining subjects received a placebo tablet which contained only saccharine and tapioca…The research protocol also required that the children attend school to take the tablets during weekends and holidays."
